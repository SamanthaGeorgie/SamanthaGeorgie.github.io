Structural Concrete

Structural concrete is used to provide support and stability for large buildings, bridges, and much more. It uses high-strength concrete as well as reinforcement materials like steel rebar or wire mesh to increase the load-bearing capacity of the structure. Structural concrete is able to withstand heavy loads, tension, and compression, making it ideal for use in construction. It is used in many buoldings at ASU to provide a safe and durable environment for students, faculty, and visitors.

McCord Hall

McCord Hall uses structural concrete columns to support the weight of the building above it. These columns have a decorative element with their diagonal placemement and are reinforced with steel rebar for added stability.

Coor Hall

Coor Hall has both concrete beams as well as an entirely concrete outer building, showcasing how the different uses of high-strength concrete can be used in a single structure.

Wilson Hall

Concrete is poured using a variety of methods including pumping, wheelbarrows, or buckets. The method used depends on both the size and location of the project.
